# Code and Conquer: The DSA Battle Arena

![Motivation](https://i.imgur.com/HQ65IQl.png)

Welcome to **Code and Conquer: The DSA Battle Arena**.

You will compete in teams to solve algorithmic challenges, think under pressure, and justify your decisions. Success will depend not only on correctness, but also on clarity, efficiency, and teamwork.


## Competition Overview

!!! tip "Purpose"
    This competition is designed to help you:

    - Apply data structures and algorithms in practice
    - Improve problem-solving under time constraints
    - Communicate technical ideas clearly
    - Collaborate effectively within a team


## Teams and Format

- You have already chosen your team
- Four teams will compete across multiple rounds
- The top two teams will advance to the final stage

!!! warning "Important"
    - Each team member must answer at least one question during the competition.  
    - Teams are free to define their own strategy: who takes the lead, who codes, who explains?

!!! tip "Competition Mindset"
    - Will your team rely on speed or precision?  
    - Will you take risks or play it safe?  
    - The right strategy can make the difference.

## Types of Challenges

Each round brings a different set of challenges. Teams must adapt, perform under pressure, and make the right decisions at the right time.  

Below are the main challenge types you will face, along with examples to help you prepare.


### 1. Quick Quiz

!!! tip "Goal"
    Test your core understanding through fast, focused questions.

**Example:**

```text
What is the time complexity of binary search?

A) O(n)  
B) O(log n)  
C) O(n log n)
```


### 2. Bug Hunt (Debugging)

!!! tip "Goal"
    Identify, analyze, and fix errors in a code snippet under time pressure.


**Example:**

```java
int sum(int n) {
    if (n == 0)
        return 1;  // incorrect base case
    return n + sum(n - 1);
}
```

**Question:** What is the issue and how would you fix it?


### 3. Explain a Concept Simply

!!! tip "Goal"
    Show true understanding by explaining a concept clearly and simply, as if teaching it to someone without a technical background.  
    Use the **Feynman technique**: break it down, use your own words, and simplify until it’s easy to grasp.

**Example:**
Explain **recursion** to a beginner in plain terms.

!!! note "What matters"
    Clarity, simplicity, and confidence in your explanation earn points; not just technical correctness.




## 4. Coding Challenge (LeetCode Style)

!!! tip "Goal"
    Solve an algorithmic problem by designing and implementing a solution, then clearly explain your approach and reasoning.

**Example:**
```text
Given an array of integers and a target value, return the indices of the two numbers that add up to the target.

Input: nums = [2, 7, 11, 15], target = 9  
Output: [0, 1]  // because nums[0] + nums[1] = 2 + 7 = 9
```

## End of Phase 1 – Advancement

This concludes the **first phase** of the competition. All teams have completed the core challenges: Quick Quiz, Bug Hunt, Explain Like I’m 5, and Coding Challenge.  

The **scores from these challenges will determine the top two teams**, who will advance to the **Final Showdown**.  

Teams that do not advance should **cheer loudly for your peers… or silently plot your comeback for next time**.  

!!! tip "Next Steps"
    The final two teams will face the **Speed Coding**, **Algorithm Debate**, and **Whiteboard Challenge**.  
    These rounds test speed, strategy, creativity, and clear reasoning under pressure.

### 5. Speed Coding

!!! tip "Goal"
    Complete a focused coding task as quickly and correctly as possible. Efficiency and accuracy are key.

**Example:**
```text
Write a function that reverses an array of integers in place.

Input: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]  
Output: [5, 4, 3, 2, 1]
```

### 6. Algorithm Debate

!!! tip "Goal"
    Justify the choice of an algorithm in a given scenario.

**Example:**

```text
Sorting a nearly sorted array:
- Team A: Insertion Sort
- Team B: Quick Sort
```

**Question:** Which approach is more appropriate and why?


### 7. Whiteboard Challenge

!!! tip "Goal"
    Think critically and explain your solution **step by step**, without writing actual code. Focus on the approach, reasoning, and efficiency.

**Example:**

```text
How would you find a target value in a sorted matrix where each row and column is in ascending order?
```

## Scoring System

Each challenge is assigned a **core point value** according to its difficulty and effort. **Bonus points** reward speed, clarity, or creativity, making the competition more dynamic.

| Challenge Type               | Core Points | Bonus Points |
|-------------------------------|------------|--------------|
| Quick Quiz                    | 5          | Fastest correct +2 |
| Bug Hunt (Debugging)          | 10         | Fastest correct +2 |
| Explain Like I’m 5            | 10         | Creativity / audience vote +3 |
| Coding Challenge (LeetCode)  | 15          | Real coders don't focus on points |
| Speed Coding                  | 10         | Speed coding is satisfying enough |
| Algorithm Debate              | 10         | Creativity / audience vote +3 |
| Whiteboard Challenge          | 10         | Clarity / reasoning +3 |

!!! tip "How it works"
    - **Core points** are awarded by the jury (a.k.a., me) for correctness and completeness.  
    - **Bonus points** reward extra effort: speed, clarity, creativity, or audience engagement.  
    - Some tasks include a **quick audience vote**. 
    - Teams are encouraged to strategize: assign members to challenges where they can maximize points.  

## Strategy Guidelines

!!! tip "Recommended approach"
    - Discuss and agree on an approach before coding
    - Prioritize correctness before optimization
    - Communicate clearly within your team
    - Consider edge cases early

!!! warning "Common pitfalls"
    - Starting implementation without a clear plan
    - Ignoring constraints or edge cases
    - Failing to justify decisions
